# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Active prop-firm traders and funded-account traders. They use this during their trading session and post-session review — journaling recent trades, tracking account health across multiple funded accounts (P1, P2, Funded phases), writing weekly/daily market outlooks, and reviewing performance metrics. The primary task on any given screen is either entering or reviewing data. Context: focused, analytical mindset; they are evaluating risk, reviewing P&L, and building accountability for their process.

## Product Purpose

NFX Hub is a professional trading SaaS for prop-firm traders. It centralizes trade journaling, multi-account tracking, market outlook writing, and performance analytics in one place. Success means a trader can open NFX Hub after a session, log their trades with full context (confluences, chart images, RR, account allocation), and leave with a clear sense of how their edge is performing over time.

## Brand Personality

Precision. Intensity. Control.

Voice: direct, confident, terse. No fluff. Numbers matter. The interface should feel like a Bloomberg terminal crossed with a modern dark-mode developer tool — every pixel serving data, not decoration.

Emotional goal: the trader should feel in command of their process. Not entertained, not impressed — *in control*.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with soft pastels, rounded marketing-card grids, and hero metrics (the "hero-metric template" slop)
- Consumer fintech apps that feel approachable and friendly (Robinhood, Coinbase aesthetic) — too casual for serious prop traders
- Cluttered Bloomberg-style interfaces with no hierarchy — we want the data density without the visual chaos
- Glassmorphism-heavy "crypto dashboard" aesthetic — blurs as primary decoration, not for emphasis
- Warm-neutral paper/parchment backgrounds — this is a dark tool, not a design portfolio

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the design** — every visual element serves information. Decoration only when it reinforces hierarchy.
2. **Precision over friendliness** — be terse, be exact. Traders value accuracy; vague affordances erode trust.
3. **Speed through familiarity** — use standard affordances (tables, tabs, modals, forms). Familiarity removes friction from a high-frequency workflow.
4. **State is signal** — wins are green, losses are red, pending is muted. Color carries semantic weight; never use accent colors decoratively.
5. **Density earns space** — pack information tightly where it serves the user; breathe only where hierarchy demands it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text meets 4.5:1 contrast on dark backgrounds. Focus indicators use the neon-green ring. Reduced-motion respected globally. No color-only encoding (losses use both red color and context label, not red alone).
